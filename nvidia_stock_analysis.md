## The Bull Case

- Hyperscalers (Meta, Microsoft, Amazon, Alphabet) committing **$475–600 billion in capex in 2026**
- Nvidia's chips remain the only game in town at scale — AMD and custom silicon are years behind in software ecosystem (CUDA moat)
- Revenue is real and growing — this isn't dot-com vaporware
- **$350 billion in Blackwell and Rubin pipeline** through 2026
- DeepSeek's January 2025 shock ultimately rebounded — capex *accelerated* on the "Jevons Paradox" argument (cheaper AI = more demand for compute)

---

## The "Buy the Rumour, Sell the News" Problem

Nvidia has a **paradox of excellence** — it keeps beating estimates and the stock keeps underreacting or falling:

- Q3 FY2026 (Nov 2025): Beat by ~$2B → stock **fell 3.15% the next day**
- Expectations are so high that beating them isn't enough
- The bar for Q4 guidance is **$70.7B** — miss that even slightly and the stock drops hard

Options markets are pricing in a **±8% swing** on earnings day.

---

## The Three Post-Earnings Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Monster Beat + Strong Guidance (~40% probability)
- Revenue >$67B AND Q1 guidance >$72B AND positive Blackwell/Rubin commentary
- **Stock reaction: +8 to +15%**

### Scenario 2: Solid Beat, Guidance In-Line (~35% probability)
- Revenue $65–67B, guidance meets but doesn't blow past $70.7B
- **Stock reaction: flat to −5%** — "sell the news"
- The market has priced a beat in; meeting expectations is disappointing

### Scenario 3: Miss or Soft Guidance (~25% probability)
- Any hint of slowing Blackwell demand, Oracle/Stargate delays, or guidance below $70B
- **Stock reaction: −10 to −20% or worse**
- Would validate the scaling/overbuilding narrative and trigger a broader AI sector selloff

## What Jensen Huang Says Matters More Than the Numbers

Watch for on the earnings call:
1. **Blackwell demand** — is the $350B pipeline real and holding?
2. **Rubin timeline** — next-gen GPU ramp speed
3. **Customer concentration** — any mention of Stargate/Oracle slowdown
4. **China export controls** — ongoing drag on revenue
5. **Gross margins** — Blackwell transition compressed them; are they recovering?

---

## The Cisco Warning

Nvidia today is Cisco in 2000:
- The **indispensable infrastructure company** of the boom
- Real revenues, real products, genuinely dominant
- Stock priced for a future that proved **smaller and slower than expected**
- When the capex cycle turned, **Cisco fell 86%**

Nvidia's stock is not a bubble in the same way as pure hype plays. But it **is** priced for perfection in a scenario that is increasingly uncertain.

---

## The Depreciation Time Bomb

Combined depreciation of just Alphabet, Microsoft and Meta:
- **$10 billion/quarter** — Q4 2023
- **$22 billion/quarter** — September 2025
- **~$30 billion/quarter** — projected end 2026

At some point CFOs ask: *"What return are we getting on this?"* When that question dominates, demand for new GPU clusters softens — and Nvidia feels it first.

## Bottom Line

| Timeframe | Most Likely Outcome |
|---|---|
| Feb 26 (day after earnings) | Volatile — ±8–15% depending on guidance |
| Q1–Q2 2026 | Elevated but under pressure as Oracle/OpenAI stress becomes visible |

The Feb 25 report is the **last unambiguous validation point** for the AI capex supercycle narrative. After that, the market will start demanding proof that the spend is generating returns.
