# What Can Oracle Do to Correct Its Data Centre Mistake?

*Analysis current as of February 2026*

---

## How Bad Is It Right Now?

| Metric | Status |
|---|---|
| Stock decline from peak | **~59%** (from ~$345 Aug 2025 to ~$142) |
| Worst quarterly performance | **Since 2001** |
| Total debt | **$108–$111 billion** |
| Free cash flow | **−$13.1 billion** |
| Projected debt by FY2028 | **$400 billion** (Morgan Stanley) |
| Legal action | **Bondholder class action** filed in Manhattan federal court |

---

## Emergency Measures Oracle Is Already Taking

### 1. Massive Layoffs — 20,000 to 30,000 Jobs

- TD Cowen (investment bank) recommends cutting **~18% of the workforce**
- Would free up **$8–10 billion annually** in cash flow
- Oracle has not confirmed the 30,000 figure publicly
- This is cost-cutting driven by **desperation, not strategy** — cannibalising the company to fund debt

### 2. Selling Cerner — The $28.3 Billion Healthcare Acquisition

- TD Cowen recommends selling Cerner to raise immediate capital
- Oracle acquired Cerner in 2022 for $28.3 billion
- A distressed sale would likely fetch **far less** than the purchase price
- Classic sign of financial stress — selling furniture to pay the mortgage

### 3. "Bring Your Own Chip" (BYOC) Model

- Asking new customers to **supply their own Nvidia hardware**
- Reduces Oracle's upfront capital expenditure
- Essentially admitting it can no longer afford to keep buying GPUs itself
- A radical reversal from its original infrastructure model

### 4. Pushing Infrastructure Costs onto Customers

- Oracle is asking **clients to contribute to building the underlying data centre infrastructure**
- Customers paying to build Oracle's own infrastructure is not a sustainable commercial model
- Signals the capital model is fundamentally broken

### 5. $45–50 Billion Capital Raise (Debt + Equity)

- $25 billion bond sale + at least $20 billion in new equity offerings
- Equity issuance **dilutes existing shareholders** significantly
- Banks are reportedly turning cautious on data centre financing — increasing borrowing costs
- Bank of America says this "defused the key risk going into 2026" — market disagrees

---

## What Oracle Should Do (But May Be Trapped From Doing)

| Option | Likelihood | Problem |
|---|---|---|
| Renegotiate OpenAI contract | Low | OpenAI needs Oracle to keep building — both trapped |
| Sell data centre assets to a REIT | Medium | Possible but locks Oracle into long leases — same debt, different form |
| Find additional hyperscaler customers | Medium | Anthropic, Meta, Google have their own infrastructure |
| Slow or pause construction | Very Low | Contractually obligated — stopping triggers penalties |
| Seek a white knight / strategic buyer | Possible | Oracle itself could become an acquisition target |
| Break up the company | Possible | Separate legacy DB/software business from AI infrastructure gamble |

---

## The Structural Trap

This is why Oracle has almost no good options:

```
Oracle builds data centres → funded by $100-400B in debt
         ↓
OpenAI contract (2027-2031) → supposed to pay for it
         ↓
OpenAI loses $74B/year → can it actually pay?
         ↓
If OpenAI can't pay → Oracle's debt has no revenue to service it
         ↓
Oracle can't easily exit → data centres are physical, immovable, illiquid assets
```

Unlike software write-downs, you **cannot delete a data centre**. Once built:
- Consumes power whether used or not
- Requires maintenance staff regardless
- Has a 20–30 year depreciation life
- Cannot easily be repurposed

---

## The Bondholder Lawsuit — Making It Worse

The class action alleges Oracle **misled investors** about the true scale of debt needed:
- September 2025: Oracle issued **$18 billion in bonds**
- October 2025: Just 7 weeks later, Oracle went back for **$38 billion more in loans**
- Bondholders claim Oracle knew it would need far more debt than disclosed
- If successful: further financial liability on top of existing debt burden
- Creates regulatory scrutiny at exactly the wrong moment
- Makes future debt issuance **more expensive and harder**

---

## Can Oracle Survive This?

**Probably yes — but not in its current form.**

Oracle's base business remains solid:
- 40-year-old company with deeply embedded database and enterprise software
- **$455 billion in Remaining Performance Obligations** (contracted future revenue)
- Cloud revenue is still growing strongly

But the Stargate bet could force:
- A **breakup** — separating legacy software from the AI infrastructure gamble
- A **forced sale** of the infrastructure division
- **Larry Ellison's exit** if the board loses confidence
- Oracle emerging as a much smaller, wounded company

---

## Most Likely Outcome

A painful multi-year deleveraging:
1. Quietly scaling back Stargate ambitions
2. Selling assets (Cerner, possibly infrastructure assets to REITs)
3. Large-scale layoffs to cut cash burn
4. Dilutive equity raises to service debt
5. Attempting to diversify away from OpenAI as sole major customer

All while trying to avoid publicly admitting the AGI bet was wrong — because admitting it accelerates the stock decline and worsens borrowing costs.
