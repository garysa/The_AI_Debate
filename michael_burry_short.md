# Michael Burry's Oracle & AI Bubble Short

*Analysis current as of February 2026*

---

## Who Is Michael Burry?

Michael Burry is the investor immortalised in *The Big Short* (book and film) for betting against the US housing market in 2007 — making $100M+ personally while the global financial system collapsed. He runs (until recently) **Scion Asset Management** and is known for identifying systemic bubbles before they pop.

He is now making the same call on the AI infrastructure bubble — with Oracle as his highest-conviction near-term target.

| Target | Position Type | Size | Status (Feb 2026) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Oracle (ORCL)** | Put options + direct short | Undisclosed | **Winning — ORCL down 59% from peak** |
| **Palantir (PLTR)** | Put options | $912M (66% of final portfolio) | **Up ~35% since Q3 2025** |
| **Nvidia (NVDA)** | Put options, $110 strike, expires Dec 2027 | $186.6M | Currently underwater |

## What Burry Said About Oracle

His exact words, posted on his *Cassandra Unchained* Substack newsletter:

> **"Oracle didn't need to do this… Now it is too late."**

He specifically cited:
- Oracle's **$300 billion OpenAI deal** as a catastrophic overcommitment
- The **Cerner acquisition** ($28.3B in 2022) as a prior mistake now compounding the crisis
- Oracle's **cloud strategy and rising debt** as fundamentally broken
- He has been **directly shorting Oracle for ~6 months** in addition to holding put options

## The Scion Closure — A Critical Signal

- Burry **closed and deregistered Scion Asset Management** in late 2025
- He did the same thing in 2008 — **after** the housing short paid off
- He launched *Cassandra Unchained* (paid Substack, $379/year) — operating **outside SEC reporting requirements**
- His final Q3 2025 13F filing disclosed the Oracle/Nvidia/Palantir shorts
- His **Q4 2025 13F was due February 14, 2026** — likely his last regulated filing

Closing the fund gives him freedom to short **without public disclosure** — meaning we may only be seeing part of his total position.

## The "Cassandra" Thesis

The newsletter name is deliberately chosen:
- In Greek mythology, Cassandra was cursed to **always be right but never believed**
- Burry's AI bubble thesis mirrors his 2007 housing thesis:

| 2007 Housing Bubble | 2025 AI Infrastructure Bubble |
|---|---|
| Enormous capital flowing into mortgage-backed securities | Enormous capital flowing into data centres |
| Core assumption: house prices never fall | Core assumption: scaling = AGI = unlimited demand |
| Counterparties all interconnected and exposed | Oracle ↔ OpenAI ↔ Nvidia all mutually dependent |
| Nobody wanted to hear the warning | AI bull narrative dominates media and markets |

## Burry's Broader AI Bubble Analysis (CNBC, November 2025)

Burry published a detailed analysis arguing:
- The AI boom is repeating the pattern of the dot-com crash of the early 2000s
- Infrastructure spending is massively outpacing actual revenue generation
- **95% of enterprise GenAI investment is yielding zero return** (MIT Media Lab)
- Companies are spending on promise, not proven ROI

## The Nvidia Short — Still Underwater

His Nvidia puts ($110 strike, expiring December 2027) represent the riskier bet:
- Nvidia is trading well above $110

- He has until December 2027 — the Feb 25 2026 earnings could be a catalyst
- With Oracle down 59%, he can afford to wait on Nvidia

## Why Oracle Is His Highest-Conviction Target

Burry identified Oracle as the most immediately vulnerable link because it has:
- The **worst debt structure** of any AI infrastructure player
- The most **concentrated customer risk** (single customer: OpenAI)
- The **least ability to exit or pivot** (physical assets, contractual obligations)
- A stock that was priced for **AGI perfection** at its peak
- A counterparty (OpenAI) that is itself loss-making

---

## The Historical Pattern

Burry closed Scion Capital in 2008 after the housing short. He reopened, rebuilt, and is now closing again after making his positions clear. This pattern suggests he believes the AI infrastructure unwind:
1. Is already well underway (Oracle down 59% validates this)
2. Will play out over months to years (Nvidia short expires Dec 2027)
3. Does not require him to manage a regulated fund to profit from it
