# Oracle's Stargate Bet: Has OpenAI Conned Oracle Into a Data Centre Trap?

*Analysis current as of February 2026*

---

## What Oracle Committed To

Oracle became a central pillar of the **Stargate Project** — a $500 billion AI infrastructure plan announced January 21, 2025 by Donald Trump, Sam Altman (OpenAI), Larry Ellison (Oracle), and Masayoshi Son (SoftBank).

| Commitment | Scale |
|---|---|
| Stargate partnership | **4.5 GW** of new data centre capacity in the US |
| Total Stargate planned capacity | **Nearly 7 GW** |
| Oracle–OpenAI contract | **$300 billion over 5 years** (2027–2031) |
| OpenAI annual obligation to Oracle | **~$60 billion/year** |
| Oracle's share of Stargate investment | **$175 billion** |
| Texas AI cluster | **450,000 Nvidia GB200 GPUs, 1.2 GW power, 1,000 acres** |

---

## The Financial Strain

| Metric | Status |
|---|---|
| Oracle free cash flow | **−$13.1 billion** (negative for first time since early 1990s) |
| Oracle total debt | **$108–$111 billion** |
| Annual CapEx guidance | **$50 billion** (~double prior year) |
| Planned 2026 capital raise | **$45–50 billion** (debt + equity) |
| Analyst estimate for total debt needed | **Up to $400 billion by FY2028** (Morgan Stanley) |
| Oracle stock decline from peak | **~59%** (from ~$345 in Sept 2025 to ~$142) |

Moody's warned of "an extended period of high leverage and negative cash flow."

---

## The Circular Dependency Problem

The entire structure only makes sense if a chain of assumptions holds:

```
Scaling LLMs → AGI capability
AGI capability → massive OpenAI revenue
OpenAI revenue → $60B/year payments to Oracle
Oracle payments → service $100-400B in debt
```

**The problem:** OpenAI is itself deeply loss-making:
- OpenAI's 2025 revenue: ~$20 billion — just **3% of total planned infrastructure spend**
- OpenAI projected operating losses in 2028: **$74 billion**
- Serious doubt that OpenAI can honour $60 billion/year in compute contracts

One analysis describes Oracle as **"trapped in an unequal relationship with OpenAI"** — Oracle takes on all the debt and construction risk while OpenAI's ability to pay depends entirely on AGI materialising.

---

## If Scaling Fails, the Maths Break

If scaling hits a wall (as growing evidence suggests):
- Demand for 7 GW of compute doesn't materialise at expected price points
- OpenAI can't generate revenue to pay Oracle
- Oracle is left holding **$100–400 billion in debt** and stranded physical assets
- DeepSeek demonstrated that cheaper, more efficient approaches can match frontier models — meaning **less raw compute is needed**, not more

---

## The Stargate Contract Is Not As Ironclad As It Sounds

- The $300 billion is not a rigid day-one commitment — deals are "typically staged with ramp schedules, minimum-use tiers and renegotiation gates"
- The OpenAI data centre leasing contract isn't expected to **hit Oracle's books until 2028**
- Shifts in the GPU market (TPUs, custom ASICs) could change the headline value
- But Oracle is **already building** — the physical and financial commitments are real

---

## Legal Exposure

Bondholders filed a **class action lawsuit** in Manhattan court on behalf of investors who bought $18 billion of Oracle debt issued in September 2025, claiming:
- Oracle **knew it would need significantly more debt** than disclosed
- Just 7 weeks after the $18B issuance, Oracle went back for another **$38 billion in loans**
- This creates regulatory scrutiny and makes future debt issuance more expensive

---

## Was Oracle "Conned"?

Perhaps not deliberately — but Ellison embraced the AGI narrative completely:
- Appeared alongside Trump and Altman to proclaim a new AI era
- Bet Oracle's balance sheet on OpenAI's AGI timeline
- That narrative is built on scaling — which is now showing serious limits
- Oracle has **no fallback** — one dominant customer (OpenAI), one thesis (scale = AGI)
